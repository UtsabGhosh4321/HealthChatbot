Q: What is malaria?
A: Malaria is a mosquito-borne disease caused by Plasmodium parasites. Common symptoms include fever, chills, headache, and fatigue. Seek medical care for testing and treatment.

Q: How can malaria be prevented?
A: Prevent mosquito bites: use bed nets, apply insect repellent, wear long sleeves, and remove standing water. In high-risk areas, follow medical advice on preventive medicines.

Q: What are the symptoms of dengue?
A: High fever, severe headache, pain behind the eyes, muscle and joint pain, nausea, vomiting, and rash. Seek medical attention if warning signs like bleeding or persistent vomiting occur.

Q: What foods are good for boosting immunity?
A: Include fruits rich in vitamin C (orange, guava), leafy greens, nuts and seeds, yogurt, and adequate protein. Hydration and sufficient sleep also support immunity.

Q: How can I reduce stress?
A: Practice deep breathing, meditation, regular exercise, maintain sleep routine, and limit caffeine. Talk to friends, family, or a counselor if stress persists.

Q: What are the symptoms of diabetes?
A: Frequent urination, excessive thirst, unexplained weight loss, fatigue, and slow-healing wounds. See a doctor for blood sugar testing.

Q: How can I prevent high blood pressure?
A: Reduce salt, maintain a healthy weight, exercise regularly, avoid tobacco and limit alcohol, manage stress, and check blood pressure periodically.

Q: Why are vaccines important?
A: Vaccines protect against serious diseases, reduce spread in the community, and help prevent outbreaks. Follow your country’s immunization schedule.

Q: What should I do for a minor burn?
A: Cool the burn under running water for 10–20 minutes, avoid ice, cover with a clean non-stick dressing. Seek care for large, deep, or facial burns.

Q: What to do in case of dog bite?
A: Wash the wound with soap and running water for 15 minutes and seek medical care immediately for anti-rabies vaccination. Observe the dog if possible and follow doctor’s advice.
